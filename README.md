# Can AI Reduce Social Media Addiction?

A research-backed AI product strategy case study applying behavioural 
psychology, harm reduction theory, and human-centred AI design to 
Compulsive social media use.

---

## Thesis

Existing digital wellbeing tools fail because they treat compulsive 
Social media use is a discipline problem requiring restriction.

This case study argues that it is a need-fulfilment problem requiring 
substitution — and proposes an AI intervention framework built on 
Harm reduction, not abstinence enforcement.

## Why This Project

Most digital wellbeing research studies either the problem or 
the intervention in isolation. This case study builds the bridge —
connecting behavioral science research directly to AI product decisions,
with every design choice traceable to a research finding.
