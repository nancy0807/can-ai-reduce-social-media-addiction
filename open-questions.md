# Open questions tracker

A running log of unresolved questions surfaced during research. The goal
is not to answer these immediately, but to keep them visible so later
phases can address them deliberately rather than by accident.

---

## From Phase 1, Section 1 — Behavioral Psychology

- What specifically does boredom need? Novelty, passive stimulation, or
  something else — and does this differ by sub-type (situational vs.
  existential boredom)?
- How does the AI detect the cue in real time without being invasive?
- What substitutes are realistic at low activation energy — i.e. require
  almost no effort to start, the way opening Instagram requires none?

## From Phase 1, Section 2 — Competitor Audit

- What does "detecting the cue" look like technically — what signals
  are realistically available (app usage patterns, time of day, device
  state) without crossing into invasive surveillance?
- How does a "need-matched substitute" avoid becoming just another
  variable-reward compulsive loop — the same trap Forest fell into?
- At what point does the AI's role taper off? What does success look
  like if the goal isn't zero usage but sustainable, intentional use?

## Cross-cutting questions (not yet assigned to a phase)

- Is "addiction" the right clinical framing, or should the case study
  formally adopt "problematic use" / "compulsive engagement" instead?
  (Flagged in early framing discussion — not yet resolved with research.)
- The redesigned-Forest insight (low-arousal, restorative substitution)
  raises a measurement question: how would a product actually measure
  "arousal level" or "cortisol-equivalent engagement" without biometric
  sensors? Self-report? Session duration patterns? This needs research
  before Phase 3 system design.
- How big is this problem, actually? Phase 1 Section 3 (market analysis)
  needs to ground the thesis in real numbers — global prevalence,
  age-group breakdown, geography — before the product scope is finalized.

---

## How this file is used

Each phase should review this list before starting and add to it as new
unknowns surface. Questions get struck through (not deleted) once
resolved, with a one-line pointer to where the answer lives.

This file is a deliberate signal of intellectual honesty for anyone
reviewing the repository — it shows which assumptions are still open
rather than presenting every claim as settled.
